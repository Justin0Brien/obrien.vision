The Science Behind Illusions

Neural Processing

How specialized cells in the visual cortex respond to different features like edges, motion, and color.

Adaptation

The temporary changes in neural sensitivity that occur after prolonged exposure to stimuli.

Context Effects

How surrounding information influences our perception of colors, sizes, and shapes.

Top-down Processing

The role of expectations, memory, and prior knowledge in shaping what we see.
